WHY JCD

This isn't charity.
It's a legacy.

John C. Dunham was a Fox Valley kid who turned Equipto into the global leader in industrial steel shelving — by out-thinking, out-building, and out-working everyone around him. He never forgot that he started on the shop floor.

JCD is his applied legacy — the belief that the next generation of makers, healers, and builders shouldn't have to go into debt to do work that matters.

We're not the Dunham Foundation. We're the offshoot built specifically for the people JCD cared most about: the kids from East Aurora to Geneva, from Batavia to Oswego East, who would rather learn a trade than sit through another gen-ed lecture.

Funded by the Dunham family. Run by Fox Valley people. Built for you.

Do I need a 4.0?
No. We don't care about your class rank. We care whether you show up, whether you can commit to the training, and whether the sector you're picking actually excites you. A 2.5 with a clear plan beats a 4.0 with no direction. Every time.
Is there an essay?
Technically there's one short-answer prompt at the end ("tell us in a few words why you want to do this"). No word count. No trick questions. No "describe a challenge you overcame." Just be yourself. Three good sentences beat three good paragraphs.
What if I'm not sure which sector yet?
Pick the one that feels closest. We'll pair you with someone working in all three so you can ask the real questions — hours, wages, what they wish they knew at 18. You can switch tracks up until your training actually starts.
What does it actually cost me?
Zero dollars. That's not marketing — that's the mechanics. JCD pays tuition direct to your program, sends a gear stipend to you, and connects you to employer partners. No fees. No hidden application cost. No catch.
What if I want to go to a 4-year school later?
Totally fine. Most of our paths include transferable college credit. A lot of scholars end up going back for a bachelor's in their late 20s — paid for by an employer. Starting in the trades isn't closing a door. It's opening a different one.
I'm undocumented. Can I still apply?
Yes. JCD scholarships are funded privately by the Dunham family — not federally — so immigration status doesn't affect eligibility. If you graduated from one of our qualifying schools and want to train into one of our three sectors, you're in the running.
How long does the application take?
Eight minutes if you move fast. [TK] Five questions, a short answer at the end, done. Save and come back if you need to. We'll confirm we got it within 48 hours.
